SECTION 1: CARDIOLOGIST / SURGERY - CARDIAC / PEDIATRIC CARDIOLOGIST
Source: Chapter 15, Section 232 - Cardiac Rehabilitation (CR) and ICR Services
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Acute myocardial infarction (heart attack) within the preceding 12 months
- Coronary artery bypass surgery
- Current stable angina pectoris
- Heart valve repair or replacement
- Percutaneous transluminal coronary angioplasty (PTCA) or coronary stenting
- Heart or heart-lung transplant
- Stable chronic heart failure

ALLOWED SERVICES:
- ECG 12 Lead
- ECHO 2D & M Mode With Doppler
- Fetal Echo / Pediatric Echo
- Cardiac Profile (CPKMB, Troponin-I)
- BNP (B-type Natriuretic Peptide)
- 48 Hours Holter
- Exercise Therapy / Cardiac Rehabilitation sessions
- Psychosocial Assessment
- Chest X-ray (for cardiac conditions)
- Pulmonary Angiography + Chest
- Private Ward with Telemetry - Cardiac Centre
- Cardiac Surgery (Surgery - Cardiac)
- Diagnostic Angiography W Supplies
- Physician prescribed exercise program
- Education and counseling for cardiac risk factors

FRAUD RULES:
- FRAUD: Cardiac Rehabilitation billed without any of the above covered conditions
- FRAUD: More than 2 one-hour sessions per day billed
- FRAUD: More than 36 sessions billed without MAC approval
- FRAUD: Cardiac services billed without physician supervision
- FRAUD: Cardiac Rehab billed by non-cardiologist without referral
- FRAUD: Same ECG interpreted and billed twice for same patient same day
- FRAUD: ECHO billed without cardiac diagnosis

SECTION 13: ALL SPECIALITIES - GENERAL DIAGNOSTIC RULES
Source: Chapter 15, Section 80 - Requirements for Diagnostic X-Ray, Lab, and Tests
================================================================================

GENERAL RULES FOR ALL DIAGNOSTIC SERVICES:

ORDERING RULES:
- All diagnostic tests must be ordered by a licensed physician or authorized NPP
- Physician assistants, nurse practitioners, and clinical nurse specialists may order tests
- Tests must be medically necessary and appropriate for the patient's condition

SUPERVISION RULES:
- Diagnostic X-rays require physician supervision
- Laboratory tests require physician order
- High-complexity tests require direct physician supervision

FRAUD RULES APPLICABLE TO ALL SPECIALITIES:
- FRAUD: Diagnostic test ordered without physician authorization
- FRAUD: Test billed that was not medically necessary for the diagnosis
- FRAUD: Same diagnostic test billed twice for same date of service
- FRAUD: Diagnostic test billed by speciality not qualified to order it
  (e.g., Brain MRI ordered by Orthopedic for back pain = FRAUD)
- FRAUD: Test results not documented in patient record but billed
- FRAUD: Upcoding - billing for higher complexity test than performed
- FRAUD: Phantom billing - billing for test never performed

BONE MASS MEASUREMENTS (Source: Section 80.5):
- Allowed for: estrogen-deficient women at risk, individuals with vertebral abnormalities,
  individuals receiving long-term glucocorticoid therapy, hyperparathyroidism,
  individuals being monitored to assess osteoporosis therapy
- FRAUD: Bone density test billed without qualifying condition
- FRAUD: Bone density test billed more than once every 2 years without exception

MASTER FRAUD DETECTION RULES - FROM CMS OFFICIAL DOCUMENTS
================================================================================

RULE 1 - MEDICAL NECESSITY (Chapter 15, Section 80):
Any service billed must be MEDICALLY NECESSARY for the patient's diagnosed condition.
Billing for unnecessary services = FRAUD.

RULE 2 - AUTHORIZED ORDERING (Chapter 15, Section 80.6):
All diagnostic tests must be ordered by an authorized physician or NPP.
Tests without valid physician order = FRAUD.

RULE 3 - NO DUPLICATE BILLING (Chapter 6, Section 20):
Same service cannot be billed twice for the same patient on the same date.
Duplicate claims = FRAUD.

RULE 4 - SPECIALITY SCOPE (Chapter 15, Section 30):
Services must be within the ordering physician's scope of practice.
Out-of-scope services = FRAUD.

RULE 5 - DOCUMENTATION (Chapter 6, Section 70.3):
All services must have corresponding documentation in patient records.
Undocumented services billed = FRAUD (Phantom Billing).

RULE 6 - SESSION LIMITS (Chapter 15, Sections 231, 232):
Rehabilitation services have maximum session limits.
Exceeding limits without approval = FRAUD.

SECTION 22: NEUROLOGIST / NEUROSURGEON
Source: NCD Chapter 1, Part 2, Section 160
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Chronic/acute intractable pain from terminal cancer or lumbar degenerative arthritis
  (for nerve tract lesions, peripheral and CNS stimulators)
- Focal epilepsy unresponsive to anticonvulsant medications
  (for stereotaxic depth electrode, VNS)
- Medically refractory partial onset seizures where surgery failed or not recommended
  (for VNS)
- Head injuries, subarachnoid hemorrhage, intracerebral hemorrhage, Reye syndrome,
  encephalopathies (for invasive intracranial pressure monitoring)
- Disuse atrophy with intact nerve supply (for NMES)
- Spinal cord injury (SCI) for walking rehabilitation (for NMES/FES)
- Partial or complete respiratory insufficiency with intact phrenic nerve (for phrenic stimulator)
- Essential tremor (ET) with disabling tremor level 3-4 on rating scale (for DBS)
- Parkinson Disease (idiopathic, at least 2 cardinal features) with persistent
  disabling symptoms despite optimal medical therapy (for DBS)
- Cerebrovascular surgery patients at risk of reduced cerebral perfusion
  (for EEG monitoring during surgery)
- Treatment-resistant depression (TRD) ONLY under CMS-approved CED clinical study (for VNS)

ALLOWED SERVICES:

160.1 - Induced Lesions of Nerve Tracts (Denervation)
- COVERED for chronic/acute pain from terminal cancer or lumbar degenerative arthritis
- Methods: surgical cutting (rhizolysis), chemical destruction, radio-frequency lesion
- Covered only in selected cases concurred by MAC medical staff

160.5 - Stereotaxic Depth Electrode Implantation
- COVERED prior to surgical treatment of focal epilepsy
- For patients unresponsive to anticonvulsant medications

160.7 - Electrical Nerve Stimulators (for Chronic Intractable Pain)
- Implanted Peripheral Nerve Stimulators: COVERED as prosthetic device
- CNS Stimulators (Dorsal Column or Deep Brain): COVERED IF:
  * Used as last resort after other treatments failed
  * Patient underwent multidisciplinary screening (psychological and physical)
  * All required facilities and personnel are available
  * Trial with temporary electrode demonstrated pain relief before permanent implant

160.8 - EEG Monitoring During Cerebral Vascular Surgery: COVERED
160.10 - Evoked Response Tests (brain stem, visual evoked response): COVERED

160.12 - Neuromuscular Electrical Stimulator (NMES)
- COVERED for disuse atrophy where nerve supply to muscle is intact
- COVERED for SCI patients for walking (FES) IF:
  * Completed minimum 32 PT sessions over 3 months
  * Intact lower motor unit (L1 and below)
  * Minimum 6 months post-spinal cord injury
  * No cardiac pacemaker, severe scoliosis, severe osteoporosis, irreversible contracture

160.14 - Invasive Intracranial Pressure Monitoring
- COVERED when medically necessary in neurosurgical/neurologic ICU
- Indicated for: head injuries, subarachnoid hemorrhage, intracerebral hemorrhage,
  Reye syndrome, encephalopathies

160.18 - Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS)
- COVERED for medically refractory partial onset seizures (surgery failed or not recommended)
- COVERED for treatment-resistant depression ONLY under CMS-approved CED study
- NOT COVERED: All other seizure types
- NOT COVERED: Depression treatment outside CED study

160.19 - Phrenic Nerve Stimulator
- COVERED for selected patients with partial/complete respiratory insufficiency
- Requires intact phrenic nerve and diaphragm

160.21 - Telephone Transmission of EEGs: COVERED when reasonable and necessary

160.24 - Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) for Essential Tremor and Parkinson Disease
- COVERED with FDA-approved device only
- ET: thalamic VIM DBS required; PD: STN or GPi DBS
- Tremor level must be 3 or 4 on Fahn-Tolosa-Marin Clinical Tremor Rating Scale
- Disabling symptoms despite optimal medical therapy required
- NOT COVERED: Non-idiopathic PD or Parkinson Plus syndromes
- NOT COVERED: Cognitive impairment, dementia, current psychosis, alcohol/drug abuse
- NOT COVERED: Structural lesions (stroke, tumor) as etiology of movement disorder
- Neurosurgeon must be trained in stereotactic procedures
- Hospital must have brain imaging (MRI/CT) and stereotactic OR equipment

NOT COVERED SERVICES - NEUROLOGY/NEUROSURGERY:
- Electric nerve stimulation for motor function disorders (MS): NOT COVERED (160.2)
- Electrotherapy for facial nerve palsy (Bell Palsy): NOT COVERED (160.15)
- Vertebral Axial Decompression (VAX-D): NOT COVERED (160.16)
- Transfer factor for multiple sclerosis: NOT COVERED (160.20)
- Sensory nerve conduction threshold tests (sNCT): NOT COVERED (160.23)
- Multiple Electroconvulsive Therapy (MECT): NOT COVERED (160.25)
- Cavernous Nerves Electrical Stimulation with Penile Plethysmography: NOT COVERED (160.26)
- TENS for chronic low back pain outside approved clinical study: NOT COVERED (160.27)

FRAUD RULES:
- FRAUD: DBS billed without FDA-approved device
- FRAUD: DBS billed for non-idiopathic PD or Parkinson Plus syndromes
- FRAUD: DBS billed for patient with cognitive impairment, dementia, or active psychosis
- FRAUD: VNS billed for depression outside CMS-approved CED study
- FRAUD: NMES/FES for SCI billed without completing required 32 PT sessions over 3 months
- FRAUD: Non-covered procedures billed (VAX-D, MECT, sNCT, Bell Palsy electrotherapy)
- FRAUD: DBS performed without multidisciplinary team involvement documented
- FRAUD: Intracranial pressure monitoring billed without documented ICU-level necessity
- FRAUD: CNS stimulator implanted without documented failure of other therapies first

SECTION 24: NUTRITIONIST
Source: NCD Chapter 1, Part 3, Section 180.1 - Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Diabetes mellitus (Type 1 or Type 2) - primary covered indication for MNT
- Renal disease (chronic kidney disease / CRD) - primary covered indication for MNT
- Change in medical condition, diagnosis, or treatment regimen requiring MNT change
  (physician must document and order additional hours)
- If physician determines both MNT and Diabetes Self-Management Training (DSMT)
  are medically necessary in same episode of care, both are covered

- MNT sessions - subsequent years: 2 hours total per year
- Additional MNT hours (beyond 3 or 2 hour limits) when physician orders and documents

- MNT for conditions other than diabetes or renal disease (at national level)
- MNT billed exceeding hour limits without physician documentation of changed condition
- MNT and DSMT billed on the same date of service
- Enteral and Parenteral Nutritional Therapy: NCD retired (Jan 1, 2022);
  coverage now at local MAC discretion

FRAUD RULES:
- FRAUD: MNT billed without diagnosis of diabetes or renal disease
- FRAUD: More than 3 hours billed in initial year without physician order for additional hours
- FRAUD: More than 2 hours billed in subsequent years without physician order for change
- FRAUD: MNT and DSMT billed on the same date of service
- FRAUD: MNT billed by non-registered dietitian/nutritionist
- FRAUD: Excessive MNT sessions billed without documented medical necessity

SECTION 25A: ENDOCRINOLOGIST - DIABETES & THYROID LAB RULES
Source: NCD Part 3, Sections 190.20, 190.21, 190.22

- Diabetes mellitus (Type 1, Type 2, gestational)
- Impaired fasting glucose / pre-diabetes / insulin resistance
- Hypoglycemia disorders (nesidioblastosis, insulinoma)
- Hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, goiter, thyroid nodules, thyroid cancer
- Metabolic disorders, hyperlipidemia, certain anemias, psychosis
- Unexplained depression, cardiac arrhythmias, disorders of menstruation
- Malnutrition, skin conditions, myalgias, alterations in consciousness

ALLOWED SERVICES:

190.20 - Blood Glucose Testing
- COVERED for diabetes management, impaired fasting glucose, insulin resistance,
  hypoglycemia disorders, catabolic/malnutrition states
- COVERED for: tuberculosis, chronic infections, alcoholism, coronary artery disease
  (women), unexplained skin conditions (pruritis, ulceration)
- Stable non-hospitalized patients: up to 4 times annually (more if clinically justified)

190.22 - Thyroid Testing (TSH, T3, T4, Free T4)
- COVERED for: hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, goiter, thyroid nodules, thyroid cancer
- COVERED for: metabolic disorders, hyperlipidemia, certain anemias, unexplained

- COVERED: up to 2 times/year for clinically stable patients
- MORE frequent testing covered if thyroid therapy altered or new symptoms appear

FRAUD RULES - ENDOCRINOLOGIST (Lab):
- FRAUD: HbA1c billed more than 4 times per year without documented justification
- FRAUD: HbA1c AND fructosamine both billed on same day after initial assay
- FRAUD: Blood glucose billed more than 4 times/year for stable non-hospitalized patient
  without documented medical necessity
- FRAUD: Thyroid tests billed more than 2 times/year for clinically stable patient
- FRAUD: Thyroid or glucose testing billed as screening for asymptomatic patients

SECTION 25D: GASTROENTEROLOGIST / GENERAL - GI LAB RULES
Source: NCD Part 3, Sections 190.32, 190.33, 190.34

- Pancreatic and biliary ductal carcinoma (for CA 19-9)
- Gastrointestinal blood loss, iron deficiency anemia, unexplained anemia
- Signs/symptoms of GI bleeding (black/red stools, abdominal pain)
- Inflammatory bowel disease, acid-peptic disease, Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis
- Colorectal carcinoma (for CEA, FOBT)

ALLOWED SERVICES:

190.32 - Gamma Glutamyl Transferase (GGT)
- COVERED for: hepatobiliary disease, drug/alcohol-induced liver injury, viral hepatitis,

- Not to be repeated after normal result unless new indications present

190.33 - Hepatitis Panel (HAAb IgM, HBcAb IgM, HBsAg, HCV Ab)
- COVERED for: differential diagnosis in patient with symptoms of liver disease
- COVERED before and after liver transplantation

- After diagnosis established: use individual tests only, not full panel

190.34 - Fecal Occult Blood Test (FOBT)
- COVERED for: evaluating known/suspected alimentary tract conditions causing bleeding,
  unexpected anemia, abnormal signs/symptoms with suspected blood loss,

- FOBT reported once for up to 3 separate specimens
- Patients on NSAIDs with GI bleeding history: no more than once every 3 months

190.30 - CA 19-9 (Tumor Marker)
- COVERED for: monitoring established diagnosis of pancreatic or biliary ductal carcinoma
- NOT covered for: diagnosing pancreatic/biliary cancer
- NOT covered for: evaluation of signs/symptoms suggestive of malignancy

FRAUD RULES - GASTROENTEROLOGY (Lab):
- FRAUD: Full hepatitis panel billed after diagnosis already established (use individual tests)
- FRAUD: CA 19-9 billed for cancer diagnosis or symptom evaluation (not covered)
- FRAUD: FOBT billed more than once per encounter (one FOBT = up to 3 specimens)
- FRAUD: FOBT billed more than once every 3 months for NSAID patients
- FRAUD: GGT billed repeatedly after normal result without new clinical indication

- Germ cell neoplasms of ovary, testis, retroperitoneum, CNS (for hCG)

190.2 - Diagnostic Pap Smear

- COVERED for: germ cell neoplasm monitoring, pregnant patients with bleeding/

- COVERED for: management and monitoring of patients with established breast cancer

SECTION 27: PREVENTION & SCREENING - UPDATED RULES
Source: NCD Part 4, Sections 210.1-210.15
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Prostate cancer screening (asymptomatic men ≥50) → Urologist
- Cervical/vaginal cancer risk (women) → OB/Gyne
- Colorectal cancer risk (asymptomatic, average risk ≥45) → Gastroenterologist
- Diabetes risk factors present → Endocrinologist
- High risk for HBV infection OR pregnant → Infectious Diseases / OB/Gyne
- HIV risk factors OR age 15-65 → Infectious Diseases
- Alcohol misuse (not dependence) → Psychiatrist / Medical Specialist
- Depression screening (adults) → Psychiatrist
- STI risk factors OR pregnant → OB/Gyne / Infectious Diseases
- Obesity (BMI ≥30) → Medical Specialist / Nutritionist
- High risk HCV OR born 1945-1965 → Infectious Diseases
- Lung cancer risk (age 50-77, smoker ≥20 pack-years) → Pulmonologist
- HIV high risk (for PrEP) → Infectious Diseases

ALLOWED SERVICES:

210.1 - Prostate Cancer Screening:
- Digital rectal examination: once every 12 months, men ≥50
- PSA blood test: once every 12 months, men ≥50
- Minimum 11 months must pass between screenings

210.2 - Pap Smear & Pelvic Examination:
- Low risk women: once every 24 months
- High risk women: more frequently than every 2 years (physician ordered)
- High risk factors: early sexual activity (<16), multiple partners (≥5),
  STD history, fewer than 3 negative paps in 7 years, DES exposure
- Pelvic exam must include at least 7 of 11 specified elements

210.2.1 - HPV Testing:
- Once every 5 years for asymptomatic women aged 30-65
- Must be done IN CONJUNCTION with Pap smear
- FDA-approved tests only, CLIA compliant lab

210.3 - Colorectal Cancer Screening:
- FOBT (guaiac or immunoassay): once every 12 months, age ≥45
- Cologuard (sDNA test): once every 3 years, age 45-85, asymptomatic,
  average risk only (no personal/family history of polyps/CRC/IBD)
- Blood-based biomarker test: once every 3 years, age 45-85, asymptomatic,
  average risk, FDA-authorized, sensitivity ≥74%, specificity ≥90%
- Screening CTC (CT colonography): NOT COVERED

210.6 - HBV Screening:
- High risk asymptomatic non-pregnant adults: covered (annually if continued risk)
- Pregnant women: at first prenatal visit + at delivery if continuing risk factors
- High risk = born in high-prevalence country (≥2%), HIV+, MSM, injection drug users,
  household/sexual contacts of HBV infected persons

210.7 - HIV Screening:
- Age 15-65: annual voluntary screening (max 1 per year)
- Outside 15-65: annual if increased risk
- Pregnant: up to 3 screenings (at diagnosis, third trimester, at labor)
- NOT covered: known HIV diagnosis, prior test within 1 year

210.8 - Alcohol Misuse Screening:
- Annual screening: covered
- Up to 4 brief face-to-face counseling sessions per year if screens positive
- Only for misuse (NOT dependence) patients
- Must be in primary care setting

210.9 - Depression Screening:
- Annual screening: up to 15 minutes, covered
- Must have staff-assisted depression care supports in place
- Primary care setting only

210.12 - Obesity Behavioral Therapy:
- BMI ≥30 required
- Week 1-4: weekly visits
- Month 2-6: biweekly visits
- Month 7-12: monthly visits (only if ≥3kg lost in first 6 months)

210.13 - HCV Screening:
- High risk (injection drug use history, blood transfusion before 1992):
  annual screening if continued risk
- Born 1945-1965: one-time screening only

210.14 - Lung Cancer LDCT Screening:
- Age 50-77, asymptomatic, ≥20 pack-year smoking history
- Current smoker OR quit within last 15 years
- Annual LDCT covered
- Must have counseling/shared decision-making visit BEFORE first LDCT
- Board-certified radiologist required
- Standardized nodule reporting system required at facility

210.15 - PrEP for HIV:
- Up to 8 counseling visits per 12 months
- Up to 8 HIV screening tests per 12 months
- Single HBV screening covered
- FDA-approved antiretroviral drugs only

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- PSA/rectal exam more than once per 12 months
- HPV testing without concurrent Pap smear
- HPV testing more than once every 5 years
- Cologuard more than once every 3 years
- Cologuard for symptomatic patients or high-risk patients
- Screening CTC (CT colonography) for colorectal cancer
- HIV screening for known HIV-positive patients
- Alcohol counseling more than 4 times per year
- Alcohol counseling for dependent (not misuse) patients
- Depression screening more than once per year
- Obesity therapy month 7-12 without ≥3kg weight loss at 6 months
- LDCT for age <50 or >77
- LDCT without prior counseling visit

FRAUD RULES - PREVENTION & SCREENING:
- FRAUD: PSA or rectal exam billed more than once in 12 months
- FRAUD: HPV test billed without same-visit Pap smear
- FRAUD: HPV test billed more than once every 5 years
- FRAUD: Cologuard billed more than once every 3 years
- FRAUD: Cologuard billed for symptomatic or high-risk patient
- FRAUD: HIV screening billed for patient with known HIV diagnosis
- FRAUD: HIV screening billed within 11 months of prior HIV screening
- FRAUD: Alcohol counseling billed more than 4 times per year
- FRAUD: Obesity therapy month 7-12 billed without documented ≥3kg weight loss
- FRAUD: LDCT billed without prior counseling/shared decision-making visit
- FRAUD: LDCT billed for patient outside age 50-77
- FRAUD: LDCT billed for non-smoker or quit >15 years ago

SECTION 28: RADIOLOGY - IMAGING POLICIES

- Refractory seizures (pre-surgical) → Neurologist (FDG PET)

- Head/CNS/spine disorders → Neurologist, Neurosurgeon (MRI)
- Disc disease → Orthopedic, Neurosurgeon (MRI)

- Obstructive sleep apnea → Pulmonologist (various imaging)
- Stress fracture, infection, tumor, myocardial viability → SPECT

ALLOWED SERVICES:

220.1 - CT Scans:
- COVERED if medically necessary for individual patient
- Must be performed on FDA-approved, full-market-release equipment
- No requirement to try other tests first (except in individual cases)
- Mobile CT: same coverage rules as stationary

220.2 - MRI:
- Head, CNS, spine: COVERED (multiple sclerosis, neuroradiology)
- Mediastinal/retroperitoneal masses, large vessel abnormalities: COVERED
- Liver, urogenital, adrenal, pelvic organs: COVERED
- Disc disease: COVERED without requiring prior radiological imaging
- Pelvic/retroperitoneal neoplasms: COVERED
- Bone neoplasm, aseptic necrosis: COVERED
- Patients with metallic prostheses (hip infection): COVERED

- MRA abdomen/pelvis (AAA repair, renal arteries): COVERED

220.2 - MRI Contraindications (NOT COVERED):

- Viable pregnancy
- Cortical bone and calcifications: NOT covered

220.4 - Mammograms:
- Diagnostic mammography: COVERED for men or women with breast symptoms,
  personal history of breast cancer, or biopsy-proven benign breast disease
- Screening mammography: NOT covered under age 35
- Age 35-39: one-time screening mammography only
- Age ≥40: annual screening (minimum 11 months between screenings)
- Must be minimum two-view exposure

220.5 - Ultrasound:
- COVERED: echocardiography, abdominal, renal, obstetric, pelvic mass,
  thyroid, breast, vascular (arterial/venous flow), pregnancy monitoring,
  placenta localization, amniocentesis guidance, biopsy guidance
- NOT COVERED: B-scan for atherosclerotic narrowing of peripheral arteries

220.6.1 - PET Heart Perfusion (Rb-82 or Ammonia N-13):
- COVERED in place of SPECT (not in addition to)
- OR after inconclusive SPECT result
- For diagnosis/management of known/suspected coronary artery disease

220.6.8 - FDG PET Myocardial Viability:
- COVERED as primary/initial study before revascularization
- OR after inconclusive SPECT
- LIMITATION: If FDG PET inconclusive, follow-up SPECT NOT covered

220.6.9 - FDG PET Refractory Seizures:
- COVERED for pre-surgical evaluation only
- To localize focus of refractory seizure activity
- NOT covered for other indications

220.6.13 - FDG PET Dementia:
- COVERED for differential diagnosis of FTD vs Alzheimer's disease
- Requirements: recent dementia diagnosis, ≥6 months cognitive decline,
  comprehensive clinical evaluation done, experienced dementia physician,
  prior SPECT/PET not already done for same indication
- NOT covered: general dementia workup without FTD vs AD differential need

220.6.17 - FDG PET Oncology:
- COVERED: initial treatment strategy for most cancers (colorectal, esophagus,
  head/neck, lymphoma, non-small cell lung, ovary, brain, small cell lung,
  soft tissue sarcoma, pancreas, testes, thyroid, breast metastasis staging,
  melanoma except regional lymph nodes, cervix except initial diagnosis)
- COVERED: up to 3 subsequent treatment strategy scans after initial therapy
- NOT COVERED: prostate cancer initial treatment strategy
- NOT COVERED: breast cancer initial diagnosis/axillary node staging
- NOT COVERED: melanoma regional lymph node initial staging

220.12 - SPECT:
- COVERED for: stress fracture, spondylosis, infection (discitis),
  tumor (osteoid osteoma), myocardial viability, ischemic vs dilated cardiomyopathy
- LIMITATION: SPECT NOT covered after inconclusive FDG PET for myocardial viability

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- MRI of cortical bone and calcifications
- MRI during viable pregnancy
- Screening mammography under age 35
- More than one screening mammography per year (age ≥40)
- SPECT after inconclusive FDG PET (myocardial viability)
- FDG PET for prostate cancer initial treatment strategy
- Screening CT colonography for colorectal cancer
- Thermography (any indication)

FRAUD RULES - RADIOLOGY:
- FRAUD: MRI billed for patient with non-MRI-conditional pacemaker
- FRAUD: MRI billed for cortical bone or calcification imaging
- FRAUD: Screening mammography billed for woman under age 35
- FRAUD: Two screening mammograms billed within 11 months
- FRAUD: PET heart perfusion billed in addition to (not instead of) SPECT
- FRAUD: SPECT billed after inconclusive FDG PET for myocardial viability
- FRAUD: FDG PET billed for prostate cancer initial staging
- FRAUD: FDG PET for refractory seizures billed for non-presurgical evaluation
- FRAUD: More than 3 subsequent FDG PET oncology scans billed without MAC approval
- FRAUD: FDG PET for dementia without documented ≥6 months cognitive decline

SECTION 30: PULMONOLOGIST - ADDITIONAL POLICIES
Source: NCD Part 4, Sections 240.1, 240.2, 240.4, 240.4.1, 240.9
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Severe emphysema (bilateral, upper-lobe predominant) for LVRS
- Chronic hypoxemia (PO2 ≤55 mmHg or SaO2 ≤88%) for home oxygen
- Obstructive Sleep Apnea (AHI/RDI ≥15, or ≥5 with symptoms) for CPAP
- Chronic respiratory failure due to COPD (PaCO2 ≥52 mmHg) for NIPPV

ALLOWED SERVICES:

240.1 - Lung Volume Reduction Surgery (LVRS):
- COVERED for severe emphysema meeting ALL criteria:
  FEV1 ≤45% predicted, TLC ≥100%, RV ≥150%, BMI ≤31.1 (men)/32.3 (women)
  PCO2 ≤60 mmHg, PO2 ≥45 mmHg
  6-min walk ≥140m post-rehabilitation
  Non-smoker ≥4 months (cotinine ≤13.7 ng/mL)
- Must have upper-lobe predominant emphysema OR non-upper-lobe with low exercise
  capacity (≤25 watts women, ≤40 watts men)
- Must be performed at Joint Commission certified LVRS facility
- Pre-op program: 16-20 sessions over 6-10 weeks required
- Post-op program: 6-10 sessions within 8-9 weeks required
- NOT COVERED: FEV1 <20%, homogeneous emphysema on CT, DLCO <20%

240.2 - Home Oxygen:
- Group I: PO2 ≤55 mmHg OR SaO2 ≤88% at rest: COVERED
- Group II: PO2 56-59 mmHg with CHF/pulmonary hypertension/erythrocythemia: COVERED
- NOT COVERED: angina without hypoxemia, breathlessness without hypoxemia,
  severe peripheral vascular disease without desaturation, terminal illness
  not affecting breathing

240.4 - CPAP for OSA:
- AHI/RDI ≥15: COVERED
- AHI/RDI 5-14 with documented excessive daytime sleepiness, cognitive impairment,
  mood disorder, insomnia, hypertension, ischemic heart disease, or stroke: COVERED
- Initial 12-week trial period
- Requires prior sleep study (PSG Type I, or HST Type II/III/IV)
- Patient education required before use

240.4.1 - Sleep Testing for OSA:
- Type I PSG (attended, sleep lab): COVERED
- Type II HST (≥7 channels, unattended): COVERED
- Type III HST (≥4 channels including airflow+respiratory+HR+O2): COVERED
- Type IV HST (≥3 channels including airflow): COVERED

240.9 - NIPPV/Home Mechanical Ventilator for COPD:
- RAD with backup rate: PaCO2 ≥52 mmHg, sleep apnea not predominant cause,
  stable COPD or post-hospitalization hypercapnia
- By 6 months: must use ≥4 hrs/day on ≥70% of days AND show PaCO2 improvement
- HMV: PaCO2 ≥52 mmHg PLUS needs FiO2 ≥36%, OR >8 hrs ventilatory support,
  OR requires alarms/battery

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- LVRS for FEV1 <20% predicted
- LVRS for homogeneous emphysema distribution on CT
- LVRS for non-upper-lobe emphysema with high exercise capacity
- Home oxygen for angina without hypoxemia
- CPAP without prior sleep study
- NIPPV continued if patient not using ≥4 hrs/day on ≥70% of days by 6 months

FRAUD RULES - PULMONOLOGIST (Additional):
- FRAUD: LVRS billed without documented pre-op rehabilitation program
- FRAUD: LVRS billed at non-certified facility
- FRAUD: LVRS billed for FEV1 <20% or homogeneous emphysema (excluded high-risk group)
- FRAUD: Home oxygen billed for angina pectoris without documented hypoxemia
- FRAUD: CPAP billed without prior sleep study documentation
- FRAUD: CPAP billed for AHI 5-14 without documented qualifying comorbidity
- FRAUD: NIPPV continued without documented ≥4 hrs/day usage compliance

SECTION 31: TRANSPLANTATION POLICIES
Source: NCD Part 4, Sections 260.1, 260.2, 260.3, 260.5, 260.9
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- End-stage liver disease (non-malignant) → General Surgeon / adult liver transplant
- Hepatocellular carcinoma (≤5cm, no macrovascular/extrahepatic spread) → liver Tx
- Biliary atresia or end-stage liver disease in children → Pediatric Surgeon
- Type I diabetes (labile, failed 12-month endocrinologist management) → pancreas Tx
- Intestinal failure (failed TPN) → General Surgeon
- End-stage heart disease → Surgery-Cardiac

ALLOWED SERVICES:

260.1 - Adult Liver Transplant:
- End-stage liver disease (all causes except non-covered malignancies): COVERED
  at CMS-approved facilities
- Hepatocellular carcinoma: COVERED if tumor ≤5cm, no macrovascular involvement,
  no extrahepatic spread, CMS-approved facility
- Follow-up care and re-transplantation: COVERED
- Immunosuppressive drugs: COVERED

260.2 - Pediatric Liver Transplant:
- Children under 18 with biliary atresia or end-stage liver disease: COVERED
- NOT COVERED: malignancy extending beyond liver margins, persistent viremia
- Must be performed at approved pediatric hospital

260.3 - Pancreas Transplant:
- Simultaneous with or after kidney transplant: COVERED
- Pancreas alone (PA): COVERED only if:
  Type I diabetes (beta cell autoantibody positive OR C-peptide ≤110% lower normal)
  AND labile DM with recurrent severe metabolic complications requiring hospitalization
  AND failed 12-month intensive endocrinologist management
  AND performed at Medicare-approved kidney transplant facility

260.5 - Intestinal/Multi-Visceral Transplant:
- COVERED for failed TPN patients at approved centers (≥10 transplants/year,
  ≥65% 1-year survival)
- TPN failure = liver failure, central vein thrombosis, frequent sepsis,
  or severe dehydration despite IV fluids

260.9 - Heart Transplant:
- COVERED at CMS-approved facilities
- Ventricular assist devices (VAD): COVERED
- Follow-up care: COVERED

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- Liver transplant for malignancies other than hepatocellular carcinoma
  (unless MAC determination for CCA, NET, HAE)
- Pediatric liver transplant with malignancy beyond liver or persistent viremia
- Pancreas alone transplant without documented failed 12-month endocrinologist care
- Islet cell transplantation outside clinical trial context
- Intestinal transplant at non-approved centers

FRAUD RULES - TRANSPLANTATION:
- FRAUD: Liver transplant billed for hepatocellular carcinoma >5cm
- FRAUD: Liver transplant billed for tumor with extrahepatic spread
- FRAUD: Pancreas alone transplant billed without 12-month endocrinologist
  management failure documentation
- FRAUD: Pancreas alone transplant billed without C-peptide or autoantibody criteria
- FRAUD: Intestinal transplant billed at facility with <10 transplants/year
- FRAUD: Islet cell transplant billed outside approved clinical trial

SECTION 32: WOUND TREATMENT POLICIES
Source: NCD Part 4, Sections 270.1, 270.3
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Stage III or IV pressure ulcers (chronic, >30 days)
- Arterial ulcers (chronic, >30 days, failed standard care)
- Diabetic ulcers (chronic, >30 days) → General Surgeon / Endocrinologist
- Venous stasis ulcers (chronic, >30 days)
- Chronic non-healing diabetic wounds for autologous PRP

ALLOWED SERVICES:

270.1 - Electrical Stimulation (ES) and Electromagnetic Therapy:
- COVERED for: Stage III/IV pressure ulcers, arterial ulcers,
  diabetic ulcers, venous stasis ulcers
- ONLY after standard wound care tried for ≥30 days with no measurable improvement
- Standard care must include: nutritional optimization, debridement,
  moist wound dressings, infection treatment, repositioning (pressure ulcers),
  glucose control (diabetic), compression (venous), circulation (arterial)
- Must show measurable healing signs within each 30-day treatment period
- Must be performed by physician, physical therapist, or incident to physician
- Wound must be evaluated monthly by treating physician

270.3 - Autologous PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma):
- COVERED for chronic non-healing DIABETIC wounds only
- Duration: up to 20 weeks coverage
- Device must be FDA-cleared for exuding cutaneous wound management
- Coverage beyond 20 weeks: MAC discretion

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- ES/Electromagnetic therapy as initial treatment (must try standard care first)
- ES/Electromagnetic therapy if no healing signs within any 30-day period
- Unsupervised ES/electromagnetic therapy
- PRP for acute surgical wounds applied to closed incision
- PRP for dehiscent wounds
- Non-autologous/homologous PDGF for chronic wounds
- Becaplermin (non-autologous growth factor)
- Infrared therapy devices for diabetic neuropathy or wounds (non-covered)
- Noncontact Normothermic Wound Therapy (NNWT): non-covered

FRAUD RULES - WOUND TREATMENT:
- FRAUD: ES/electromagnetic therapy billed without 30-day standard care trial
- FRAUD: ES/electromagnetic therapy continued without documented healing signs
  in each 30-day period
- FRAUD: Unsupervised wound ES billed as physician/therapist service
- FRAUD: PRP billed for acute surgical wounds or dehiscent wounds
- FRAUD: PRP billed for non-diabetic chronic wounds (without MAC approval)
- FRAUD: PRP billed beyond 20 weeks without MAC authorization
- FRAUD: Infrared therapy billed for diabetic neuropathy or wounds

SECTION 33: MEDICAL SUPPLIES & DME POLICIES
Source: NCD Part 4, Sections 280.3, 280.4, 280.6, 280.7, 280.8, 280.14
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Mobility deficit impairing daily activities → Physical Med & Rehab / Orthopedic
- Severe arthritis (hip/knee) or neuromuscular disease → Seat lift
- Lymphedema (after 4-week conservative trial) → Pneumatic compression
- Chronic venous insufficiency with venous stasis ulcers (after 6-month trial)
- Positioning needs OR special attachment needs → Hospital bed
- Stage III/IV pressure sores, bedridden → Air-fluidized bed
- Type I diabetes (CSII pump criteria) → Endocrinologist
- Severe spasticity unresponsive to oral medications → Neurologist
- Severe chronic intractable pain (malignant/non-malignant) → relevant speciality
- Primary hepatocellular carcinoma or colorectal liver metastases → General Surgeon

ALLOWED SERVICES:

280.3 - Mobility Assistive Equipment (MAE):
- Canes, crutches, walkers: COVERED if mobility deficit impairing daily activities
- Manual wheelchairs: COVERED if sufficient upper extremity function
- Power wheelchairs: COVERED if manual wheelchair insufficient
- Scooters/POVs: COVERED if sufficient strength/postural stability
- Clinical algorithmic assessment required

280.4 - Seat Lift:
- COVERED for severe arthritis (hip/knee) OR muscular dystrophy/neuromuscular disease
- Must show therapeutic benefit, part of physician treatment plan
- Smooth operation type only (NOT spring-release/catapult type)

280.6 - Pneumatic Compression Devices:
- Lymphedema: COVERED after 4-week conservative trial (compression bandage,
  exercise, elevation) with no significant improvement
- CVI with venous stasis ulcers: COVERED after 6-month conservative trial failure
- Physician oversight required (evaluation, prescription, monitoring)

280.7 - Hospital Beds:
- COVERED if patient needs positioning OR special attachments not feasible in
  ordinary bed
- Variable height: severe arthritis, cardiac conditions, spinal cord injuries,
  stroke, amputees

280.8 - Air-Fluidized Bed:
- Stage III or IV pressure sore required
- Patient must be bedridden/chair bound
- Without bed, patient would require institutionalization
- Must have failed ≥1 month conservative treatment
- Trained adult caregiver required at home
- Monthly physician re-evaluation and recertification required
- NOT COVERED: coexisting pulmonary disease, wet soaks without occlusive covering,
  caregiver unable to provide care, structural support insufficient

280.14 - Infusion Pumps:
External pumps COVERED for:
- Iron poisoning (deferoxamine)
- Thromboembolic disease/pulmonary embolism (heparin, institutional only)
- Liver cancer chemo (primary hepatocellular or colorectal unresectable)
- Intractable cancer pain (morphine)
- CSII (insulin pump) for Type I diabetes meeting strict criteria:
  C-peptide ≤110% lower normal OR beta cell autoantibody positive
  PLUS Criterion A (≥6 months multiple daily injections, HbA1c >7% or other) OR
  Criterion B (prior pump user before Medicare enrollment)

Implantable pumps COVERED for:
- Liver cancer (hepatic artery 5-FUdR, unresectable)
- Severe spasticity (intrathecal baclofen, after failed oral medications)
- Chronic intractable pain (intrathecal/epidural opioids, life expectancy ≥3 months)

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- Implantable insulin pump for diabetes
- Implantable heparin pump for thromboembolic disease
- Vancomycin via external infusion pump (DME benefit)
- Air-fluidized bed with coexisting pulmonary disease
- Seat lift (spring-release/catapult type)
- MAE without documented clinical assessment of mobility deficit

FRAUD RULES - MEDICAL SUPPLIES & DME:
- FRAUD: Pneumatic compression for lymphedema without documented 4-week
  conservative trial failure
- FRAUD: Pneumatic compression for CVI without documented 6-month trial failure
- FRAUD: Air-fluidized bed billed without documented 1-month conservative
  treatment failure
- FRAUD: Air-fluidized bed billed for patient with coexisting pulmonary disease
- FRAUD: Air-fluidized bed billed without monthly physician recertification
- FRAUD: CSII pump billed without documented C-peptide criteria or autoantibody
- FRAUD: CSII pump billed without ≥3-month physician follow-up visits
- FRAUD: Implantable pump for diabetes (insulin) - never covered
- FRAUD: Implantable baclofen pump without documented 6-week oral medication trial
- FRAUD: Power wheelchair billed without clinical algorithmic assessment

SECTION 34: NEPHROLOGIST - LEVOCARNITINE POLICY
Source: NCD Part 4, Section 230.19
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Carnitine deficiency in ESRD patients on dialysis
- EPO-resistant anemia (hematocrit <30% despite standard EPO + iron)
- Intradialytic hypotension (≥2 episodes in 30 days despite standard measures)

ALLOWED SERVICES:

230.19 - Levocarnitine for ESRD:
- COVERED for: carnitine deficiency (plasma free carnitine <40 micromol/L)
  WITH EPO-resistant anemia OR intradialytic hypotension
- Patient must have been on dialysis minimum 3 months
- Continued coverage: requires documented improvement within 6 months
- All other levocarnitine indications in ESRD: NOT COVERED

NOT COVERED SERVICES:
- Levocarnitine for ESRD patients on dialysis <3 months
- Levocarnitine without documented plasma carnitine <40 micromol/L
- Levocarnitine without EPO-resistant anemia or intradialytic hypotension
- Continued levocarnitine without improvement within 6 months

FRAUD RULES - NEPHROLOGIST (Levocarnitine):
- FRAUD: Levocarnitine billed for ESRD patient on dialysis <3 months
- FRAUD: Levocarnitine billed without documented carnitine level <40 micromol/L
- FRAUD: Levocarnitine continued beyond 6 months without documented improvement
- FRAUD: Levocarnitine billed for indication other than anemia or hypotension

SECTION 35: CLINICAL TRIALS - ALL SPECIALITIES
Source: NCD Part 4, Section 310.1
================================================================================

310.1 - Routine Costs in Clinical Trials:
- Routine costs of qualifying clinical trials: COVERED
- Items/services used to diagnose/treat complications from trial participation: COVERED
- Conventional care items otherwise covered by Medicare: COVERED
- Items required solely for provision of investigational item: COVERED
- Monitoring of investigational item effects: COVERED
- Treatment of complications from investigational items: COVERED

NOT COVERED in Clinical Trials:
- The investigational item/service itself (unless otherwise covered)
- Items provided solely for data collection not used in patient management
- Items customarily provided free by research sponsors

FRAUD RULES - CLINICAL TRIALS:
- FRAUD: Billing investigational item itself as routine cost
- FRAUD: Billing data-collection-only items (e.g., extra CT scans not clinically needed)
- FRAUD: Billing items provided free by research sponsor

================================================================================
MASTER FRAUD DETECTION RULES - UPDATED (All Specialities)
================================================================================

RULE M1 - FREQUENCY VIOLATION:
Any service billed more frequently than policy-specified limit is fraud.
Examples: PSA >1/year, HPV >1/5years, Cologuard >1/3years, mammogram >1/year

RULE M2 - PREREQUISITE NOT MET:
Any service billed without required prior treatment trial is fraud.
Examples: PUVA without conventional psoriasis tx, CPAP without sleep study,
pelvic stimulator without PME trial, ES without 30-day standard wound care

RULE M3 - WRONG PATIENT CRITERIA:
Service billed for patient not meeting clinical criteria is fraud.
Examples: LVRS with FEV1 <20%, pancreas Tx without Type I DM criteria,
levocarnitine without carnitine deficiency, PRP for acute wounds

RULE M4 - NON-COVERED SERVICE:
Billing explicitly non-covered service is fraud.
Examples: implantable insulin pump, screening CTC, thermography,
NNWT, infrared therapy for wounds, SPECT after inconclusive FDG PET

RULE M5 - FACILITY NOT APPROVED:
Procedure performed at non-approved facility billed as covered is fraud.
Examples: LVRS at non-Joint-Commission certified facility,
intestinal transplant at <10 transplants/year center

RULE M6 - MISSING DOCUMENTATION:
Service billed without required documented evidence is fraud.
Examples: IVIg without biopsy-proven diagnosis, cryosurgery salvage
without radiation failure documentation, PRP beyond 20 weeks without MAC auth

SECTION 36: GENERAL PHYSICIAN / ANY LICENSED PHYSICIAN
Source: Medicare Benefit Policy Manual - General Practice Guidelines
Note: These services are permitted for ANY licensed physician regardless of
specialty. All 35 specialties in this system inherit these permissions.
================================================================================

ALLOWED CONDITIONS (Diagnoses):
- Fever / Pyrexia of unknown origin
- General weakness / Fatigue
- Headache (non-neurological, screening)
- Common cold / Upper respiratory tract infection (URTI)
- Influenza / Flu
- Nausea / Vomiting / Diarrhea (general)
- Dehydration
- Hypertension (screening and basic monitoring)
- Diabetes mellitus (basic monitoring)
- Anemia (screening)
- Nutritional deficiency (Vitamin D, B12, Iron)
- General pain / Body ache
- Weight loss / Weight gain (unexplained)
- Pre-operative workup (any surgery)
- Routine health screening / Annual checkup
- Infection (general, unspecified)
- Inflammatory condition (general)
- Thyroid disorder (basic screening)

ALLOWED SERVICES:
- Complete Blood Count (CBC)
- Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)
- Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)
- Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)
- C-Reactive Protein (CRP)
- Blood Glucose (Fasting / Random)
- HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)
- Urine Routine Examination (Urine R/E)
- Urine Culture / Sensitivity (Urine C/S)
- Liver Function Tests (LFT)
- Kidney Function Tests (KFT) / Renal Profile
- Serum Electrolytes (Sodium, Potassium, Chloride)
- Serum Creatinine
- Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)
- Uric Acid
- Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH)
- Vitamin D (25-OH)
- Vitamin B12
- Iron Studies (Serum Iron, TIBC, Ferritin)
- Lipid Profile (Cholesterol, Triglycerides, HDL, LDL)
- Chest X-ray 1 View (basic screening)
- ECG 12 Lead (basic screening, pre-operative)
- Blood Pressure Monitoring
- Temperature / Pulse / Oxygen Saturation (vital signs)
- General Physician Consultation
- Follow-up Consultation
- Paracetamol / Antipyretics (fever management)
- Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORT)
- IV Fluids (basic, for dehydration)
- Blood Culture (for fever workup)
- Widal Test (typhoid screening)
- Dengue NS1 / IgG / IgM
- Malaria Parasite Test (MP Test)
- Hepatitis B Surface Antigen (HBsAg) - screening
- Hepatitis C Antibody (Anti-HCV) - screening
- HIV screening (with consent)
- Random Blood Sugar (RBS)
- Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS)
- Peripheral Blood Film (PBF)
- Prothrombin Time (PT) / INR - pre-operative
- APTT - pre-operative

FRAUD RULES:
- FRAUD: Specialty-specific advanced procedure billed under general physician code
- FRAUD: General physician services billed without any documented clinical indication
- FRAUD: Routine screening tests billed as diagnostic workup without symptoms
- FRAUD: Pre-operative tests billed without documented upcoming surgical procedure
- FRAUD: Duplicate billing for same general physician consultation same day
- FRAUD: Advanced imaging (MRI, CT scan) billed by general physician without referral or specialist indication
- FRAUD: Specialist-only procedures billed as general physician service
